Working for Climate is founded by the late Martin Mafereka, Joel Letsipa and Motlatsi Makhasane in 16 June 2009 as part of launching the youth month. To be an answer to educating our nation around climate change, global warming and green economy. It was formally registered in 2017 and an incubatee of Centre For Entrepreneurship at Tosa Campus incubation programme. Working For Climate advocated and lobbied for the regulation of climate change which saw a climate change bill passed in June 2018, now realising a green industrial park. Working For Climate has won an award from Youth Chamber Of Commerce and Industry Free State


Mission Statement

This initiative will not only be a drive for a mass recruitment campaign and entrepreneur participation, supplementing our government programs, will further also be the answer to our manifesto and creating 6 million works opportunities by 2019. Many of which will be of a long duration.This phase of our democratic transition calls for bold and decisive steps to place the economy on a qualitative difference path that eliminates poverty and unemployment creates sustainable livelihoods and substantially reduces inequality. The National Development Plan (NDP) aims to eradicate poverty, increase employment and reduce inequality by 2030.The NDP lays the foundation for long term planning for this radical socio-economic agenda over the next 20 years. Its implementation will be a people-drive process to which everyone is able to contribute. Where appropriate, the plan will be adapted to meet changing condition and incorporate key learning.

What is needed to achieve progress

1 Progress cannot be achieved without strong partnerships with other departments, and work occurs across policy, planning and implementation levels

2 At planning level department working to ensure that climate change should prioritise, include discussions about the country's energy mix

3 To support adaptation planning, the Long Term Adaptation Scenerios project produce a series of impact assessments for particular sectors, establish priority interventions, and identify responses that require co-ordination across different sectors

4 Detailed work should now be done on areas that are particularly vulnerable. Adaptation should also include one of the country's contributions under the new climate change agreement that is being negotiated

5 With regards to mitigation, the intention is ultimately to develop a set of emission reduction pathways. Emission reduction will be achieved through a range of tools, including treasury's carbon tax and mitigation plans for sectors and sub-sectors
