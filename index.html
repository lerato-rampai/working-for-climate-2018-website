Climate Change in South Africa

The environment is often described as the surrounding within which humans exist and which are made up of bio-geo-physical, social and economic components and the Interrelationship and connection between them.

South Africa faces major changes to it's climate. Our average annual temperatures have increased by at least 1.5 times more than the observed global average of 0.65oC in the past 50 years. Extreme rainfall events have increased in frequency, climate change poses a significant threat to South Africa's water resources, food security, health, infrastructure, ecosystem services and biodiversity.
